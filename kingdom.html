Imperial Chronicle

The Kingdom

Khanid Kingdom lore, the Dark Amarr, and the rebellion that forged a sovereign power.

The Heir Who Refused

A few centuries ago, when the last Amarr Emperor was chosen, Khanid stood among the royal heirs. Ancient tradition demanded that those who failed to claim the Throne surrender themselves to ritual death. Khanid refused.

Where others saw duty, he saw extinction. Where the court expected obedience, he chose dominion. His vast domains split from the Empire, and a new power took shape beneath black banners and silver steel.

The Schism and the War

The Amarr Empire retaliated. Imperial force struck against vulnerable outer regions, tearing away holdings and punishing defiance. Yet the Kingdom did not collapse. The core endured, hardened by siege, pride, and necessity.

The Kingdom survived not because it was stronger than the Empire, but because it was too stubborn, too useful, and too disciplined to die.

The Dark Amarr

Outsiders call them the Dark Amarr. The name is not merely a reference to black hulls or shadowed courts. It describes a civilization that preserved Amarrian faith, hierarchy, nobility, and ritual while embracing the customs of others, chiefly the Caldari.

Visitors often describe the Kingdom as a surreal fusion: cathedrals beside megacities, scripture beside balance sheets, royal pride beside military pragmatism.

Caldari Influence

The Kingdom learned that survival required more than devotion. It required supply lines, contracts, efficient command, practical weapons, and the willingness to replace beautiful dogma with useful machinery when necessary.

That is why Khanid design is so distinctive: Amarr armor and authority fused with missile doctrine and utilitarian menace.

The Karnivale Dossier

Analysts disagree on the origins of Khanid Karnivale. Some describe it as a gathering of veteran militia capsuleers, religious extremists, and former naval personnel whose loyalties remain deliberately ambiguous.

Every Karnivale vessel bears the prefix KK, commonly rendered as King Khanid’s. The prefix differs from the Royal Khanid Navy’s RKN, leading some observers to speculate that the Chapter’s relationship to the Crown may be closer than admitted.
