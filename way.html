Doctrine

The Khanid Way

Competence over ego. Style with purpose. Faith with teeth.

Independence

The Kingdom was born when Khanid refused to die for a tradition that would have ended his destiny. The Karnivale respects loyalty, but it does not worship passivity.

Competence

Ritual has its place. So does winning. A pilot who learns, adapts, and returns with the hull intact honors the Chapter more than one who merely speaks loudly.

Beauty as Warfare

A ship should be dangerous, yes. But it should also announce that its pilot chose the fight, the hull, the skin, and the myth on purpose.

Good Fights

Statistics are useful. Stories are better. The Karnivale values memorable engagements, brave pilots, and wreckage worth talking about.

Real Life First

No pilot is expected to burn out for an internet spaceship cult, no matter how magnificently dressed that cult may be.

Roleplay Welcome

Speak as a loyal subject, a cynical officer, a holy lunatic, or a practical shipwright. The Chapter has room for character.

Fly Dangerous. Fly Beautiful. Fly Khanid.
